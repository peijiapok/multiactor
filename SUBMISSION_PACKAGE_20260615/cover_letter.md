Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, “Multi-actor acceptability screening of EV fleet charging under behavioral stress: congestion-game diagnosis and incentive analysis,” for publication in Applied Energy.

The manuscript addresses a deployment problem in managed electric-vehicle charging: policies that appear effective under single metrics such as delivered energy, cost, queue activity, or peak demand may not be acceptable simultaneously to drivers, fleet or facility operators, and grid operators. We develop a multi-actor acceptability-gate framework and evaluate EV fleet charging control under capacity limits, behavior stress tests, grid-policy interventions, and a representative IEEE-33 feeder stress screen.

The main contribution has two parts: a multi-actor acceptability-screening framework, and a calibrated mechanism analysis built on it. In the tested weekly severity sweep, service-oriented charging passes all actor gates under full compliance, while low-deviation behavior reduces same-episode all-pass acceptability to near zero under the specified gates; a targeted 28-day check supports this transition, and a demanded-kWh trace shows the behavioral effect is request-event churn rather than increased simulator demanded kWh. Building on the screen, we use a calibrated congestion-game overlay to interpret the collapse as an uncoordinated compliance equilibrium and to test, within the model, whether a bounded operator incentive can move the system back toward a jointly acceptable operating point. We are deliberately disciplined about this layer: it is a mechanism analysis on a soft-calibrated behavioral model with normalized-utility welfare, not a field-validated incentive design, and the manuscript states these bounds explicitly.

We believe the manuscript fits Applied Energy because it connects EV charging control, behavioral response risk, and distribution-grid stress screening into a reproducible applied energy-system evaluation. The manuscript avoids unsupported claims of optimality, field-calibrated behavior, or feeder validation, and includes explicit limitations and reproducibility boundaries.
